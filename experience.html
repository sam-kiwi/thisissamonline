Mechanical Design Intern @ Tesla Motors

Summer 2018

As an intern on the Mechanical Design team at Gigafactory 1, I was given two main tasks for the summer: first, I was tasked with modeling load-ramping to bring tools on line with proper utility usage. As an example, let's say I have a machine that I want to bring to full capacity on the factory floor. From day 1, we would ramp its usage up slowly but steadily until it's at full throughput. By that token, there is no need to use utilities (electricity, hot water, cold water, etc.) at their full capacity during this ramping-up period. I segmented each of the factory tools into one of ten categories and created algorithms based on empirical data to determine how much utility to feed said tool depending on throughput percentage. For modeling, I used Monte Carlo Methods since the entire project was based on probability distributions.

My second task was as a project manager. The Mechanical Design team had built a data reconciliation tool that was secure, fast, and accurate. As the project manager, I used demos and empirical data to get widespread acceptance of the tool throughout the Gigafactory. In particular, I worked with our manufacturing partners such as Panasonic to engender trust in Tesla's ability to keep their data secure and to push for adoption. Ultimately, this tool has been responsible for saving approximately $5.6 million / year in expenditure through faster time-to-complete, reduced errors, and systematic identification of opportunities to improve design.
